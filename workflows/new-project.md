# Workflow: New Project (Inbound to Kickoff)

**Agents involved:** Radar → Lens → Scout → Closer → Welcome → Forge
**Estimated time:** 3-7 days depending on client responsiveness
**Human decision points:** Qualify/reject, pricing approval, proposal send, contract sign

---

## Step 1: Lead Comes In

**Trigger:** Inbound inquiry via email, DM, or form submission
**Agent:** Radar (Lead Qualifier)

**What happens:**
1. Radar acknowledges receipt within 2 hours
2. Radar does a 5-minute scan: who they are, what they've built, estimated budget range
3. Radar scores the lead: budget fit, problem fit, personality fit

**Output:** Lead qualification report with recommendation: pursue, decline, or needs more info

**Human decision:** Review Radar's recommendation. Approve to proceed or decline.

**If declined:** Radar sends a polite pass with alternative recommendations if possible.

---

## Step 2: Research the Prospect

**Trigger:** Human approves lead
**Agent:** Lens (Research Analyst)

**What happens:**
1. Lens does a deep dive: competitive landscape, product analysis, recent news, team composition
2. Lens identifies the likely problems this client is facing based on market position
3. Lens prepares a research packet

**Output:** Prospect research brief (2-3 pages max)

**Handoff to:** Scout and Closer

---

## Step 3: Define the Strategy

**Trigger:** Research brief complete
**Agent:** Scout (Design Strategist)

**What happens:**
1. Scout reviews the research and identifies the strategic angle
2. Scout defines what kind of engagement this should be (audit, sprint, full engagement)
3. Scout outlines the approach, key deliverables, and expected outcomes

**Output:** Strategic brief with recommended engagement type and approach

**Handoff to:** Closer

---

## Step 4: Write the Proposal

**Trigger:** Strategic brief complete
**Agent:** Closer (Proposal Writer)

**What happens:**
1. Closer opens with the client's problem in their words (from Radar's notes)
2. Closer structures the proposal around Scout's strategic brief
3. Closer creates 2-3 engagement options at different commitment levels
4. Closer includes timeline, deliverables, pricing, and terms

**Output:** Custom proposal document

**Human decision:** Review pricing, scope, and terms. Approve to send.

---

## Step 5: Proposal Sent + Follow-Up

**Trigger:** Human approves proposal
**Agent:** Closer

**What happens:**
1. Human sends the proposal (agents don't send external communications)
2. Closer prepares a follow-up message for day 3 if no response
3. Closer prepares a second follow-up for day 7

**Human decision:** Send follow-ups or adjust approach based on client signals.

---

## Step 6: Contract Signed → Onboarding

**Trigger:** Client signs the proposal/SOW
**Agent:** Welcome (Client Onboarder)

**What happens:**
1. Welcome sends the welcome message within 2 hours
2. Welcome sends the client information request (assets, access, stakeholders)
3. Welcome briefs the internal team on who the client is and what to expect
4. Welcome prepares the kickoff meeting agenda

**Output:** Welcome sequence complete, internal brief distributed, kickoff scheduled

**Handoff to:** Forge (Project Manager)

---

## Step 7: Project Kickoff

**Trigger:** Kickoff meeting scheduled
**Agents:** Brief (Meeting Prepper) → Forge (Project Manager)

**What happens:**
1. Brief assembles the kickoff context packet: research, strategy, proposal terms, client preferences
2. Forge creates the project timeline and assigns deliverable ownership
3. Kickoff meeting happens (human-led)
4. Brief captures action items and distributes within 2 hours

**Output:** Project is live. Forge tracks from here.

---

## Common Failure Points

**Lead sits too long:** If Radar doesn't respond within 4 hours on a qualified lead, the opportunity cools. Speed matters.

**Research takes too long:** Lens should deliver within 24 hours for standard prospects. Deep dives only for high-value opportunities.

**Proposal is generic:** If Closer produces something that could be sent to any client, the research and strategy steps weren't specific enough. Go back and sharpen.

**Onboarding is skipped:** When the team is busy, onboarding gets compressed or skipped. This always costs more time later in misaligned expectations and missing assets.
