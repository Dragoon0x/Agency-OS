# Workflow: Content Production (Idea to Distribution)

**Agents involved:** Lens → Signal → Ink → Proof → Kit → Pulse → Echo
**Cadence:** Weekly (newsletter) + daily (social)
**Human decision points:** Topic approval, final draft approval, publish

---

## Step 1: Find the Topic

**Trigger:** Weekly content planning (every Monday)
**Agent:** Lens (Research Analyst)

**What happens:**
1. Lens scans: what's happening in the industry, what conversations are active, what questions keep coming up
2. Lens surfaces 3-5 potential topics with a one-line hook for each
3. Lens flags if any topic connects to a current client engagement (anonymization needed)

**Output:** Topic shortlist with supporting data points

**Human decision:** Pick the topic for this week.

---

## Step 2: Draft the Newsletter

**Trigger:** Topic approved
**Agent:** Signal (Newsletter Editor)

**What happens:**
1. Signal structures the piece: one core idea, supporting evidence, practical takeaway
2. Signal writes the draft in the studio's voice
3. Signal writes the subject line last

**Output:** Newsletter draft (500-800 words)

**Human decision:** Review draft. Approve, request edits, or redirect.

---

## Step 3: Polish the Copy

**Trigger:** Draft approved (or after revisions)
**Agent:** Ink (Copywriter)

**What happens:**
1. Ink reviews for voice consistency, clarity, and punch
2. Ink tightens sentences, kills filler, sharpens the opening
3. Ink reads it out loud (metaphorically) — if it sounds like a press release, rewrites

**Output:** Polished newsletter draft

**Handoff to:** Proof

---

## Step 4: Quality Check

**Trigger:** Polished draft ready
**Agent:** Proof (QA Reviewer)

**What happens:**
1. Proof checks: spelling, grammar, factual claims, links, formatting
2. Proof verifies no confidential client information leaked
3. Proof checks subject line against body content (does it match?)

**Output:** QA report — pass or issues flagged

**If issues:** Back to Ink or Signal for fixes. Then re-review.

---

## Step 5: Create Visuals

**Trigger:** Copy passes QA
**Agent:** Kit (Asset Producer)

**What happens:**
1. Kit creates the newsletter header image
2. Kit creates the OG image for social sharing
3. Kit sizes assets for each distribution platform

**Output:** Newsletter visual assets, social-ready graphics

---

## Step 6: Publish

**Trigger:** Copy approved + visuals ready
**Human action:** Hit publish on newsletter platform

---

## Step 7: Repurpose for Social

**Trigger:** Newsletter published
**Agents:** Pulse (Twitter Strategist) + Echo (Content Repurposer)

**What happens:**
1. Pulse extracts the sharpest take from the newsletter and writes a tweet or thread
2. Echo adapts the core idea for LinkedIn, Instagram carousel, or other platforms
3. Kit produces any additional platform-specific visuals

**Output:** 3-5 pieces of content across platforms from one newsletter

**Human decision:** Review and approve each platform post before publishing.

---

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Action | Agent |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Monday | Topic research + selection | Lens → Human |
| Tuesday | Newsletter draft | Signal |
| Wednesday | Copy polish + QA | Ink → Proof |
| Thursday | Visuals + final approval | Kit → Human |
| Thursday/Friday | Publish + social distribution | Human → Pulse + Echo |

---

## Common Failure Points

**Topic is too broad:** If Signal can't find one core idea, the topic isn't focused enough. Go back to Lens and narrow.

**Draft sounds generic:** Usually means Signal didn't have enough specific material. Feed more research or real examples.

**Social feels like a summary:** Pulse and Echo should create native content, not compressed versions of the newsletter. If the tweet reads like a TL;DR, it's not working.

**Inconsistent publishing:** The schedule is a promise to subscribers. If you skip weeks, build a two-issue buffer. Signal can draft ahead when inspiration is high.
